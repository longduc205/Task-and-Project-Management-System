- Hit the targeted budget cost.
- Finish within scheduled project time.
- Meet all required performance specs.
- Goal: Hit this 3D target.

## The Cost Escalation Problem

- Actual costs often exceed estimates.
- Early projects lack detailed information.
- Estimators show extreme over-optimism.
- "Buy in" deliberately lowers bids.
- Escalation is a normal risk.

## The Cone of Uncertainty Narrows Over Time

You cannot have a perfect estimate on day one. Estimate accuracy is a function of the project life cycle.

## Conception Phase

The 'region of uncertainty' is massive. Estimates are largely guesstimates based on high-level standards.

## Definition Phase

As the system and work activities are nailed down, material costs and labor times become clearer.

## Execution Phase

The baseline is locked. Changing decisions here is incredibly costly due to sunk costs.

## Accuracy vs. Precision

- Accuracy means closeness to actuals.
- Precision means exact decimal points.
- Goal: Maximize actual estimate accuracy.
- Use PERT three-point estimates.
- Combines optimistic, pessimistic, and likely.

## Project Cost Accounting Systems

- PCAS tracks all project costs.
- Aggregates work package cost estimates.
- Tracks actual versus budgeted costs.
- Quickly identifies any cost variances.
- Essential for large IT projects.

![Image](/Users/azure_awabi/Downloads/Task-and-Project-Management-System/markdown_docs/05 Cost Estimating and Budgeting_artifacts/image_000022_a1dacca5a2bcc17026aaab7437cf7d9ac98ab047745fa2b616ea753e0b2b6bf7.png)

## Life Cycle Costs (LCC)

- Looks beyond project execution phase.
- Cradle-to-grave system costs.
- Includes operations and maintenance costs.
- Maintenance costs often exceed project.
- Decisions today impact future costs.

![Image](/Users/azure_awabi/Downloads/Task-and-Project-Management-System/markdown_docs/05 Cost Estimating and Budgeting_artifacts/image_000023_20d2e4977771930769ea6525f19dd2d6a2723d3f202c2dc0c40793db5a75b9d0.png)
