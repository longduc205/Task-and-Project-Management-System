## INS3044 IT Project Management: Final Project Information

Group size: 2-4 students

Weight: 60% of the total course grade

## 1. Project Overview

The final project for INS3044 requires students to apply the principles of IT Project Management to a real-world scenario. Your team will act as a project management office (PMO) responsible for planning, executing, and documenting the development of an IT system. You are required to produce a comprehensive Project Management Plan (PMP) and a functional prototype of the proposed system.

## 3. Detailed Requirements &amp; Deliverables

## A. Final Project Report (The Management Plan)

Your report must follow professional standards and include:

- Project Scope and Objectives: Clear definition of "In-Scope" and "Out-of-Scope" items. SMART objectives for the project.
- Work Breakdown Structure (WBS): A hierarchical decomposition of the total scope of work (minimum 3 levels deep).
- Timeline and Milestones: A detailed schedule (Gantt Chart) including dependencies and key milestones.

## · Resource Management Plan:

- o Human Resources (Roles, Responsibilities/RACI matrix).
- o Materials/Infrastructure requirements.
- o Estimated Budget and Cost baseline.
- Risk Management Plan: Risk Register identifying at least 5 potential risks, impact assessment, and mitigation strategies.
- Communication &amp; Collaboration Plan: Documented communication channels, meeting schedules, and how tools (JIRA/Trello/MS Project) were used.

## B. System Prototype

While this is a Management course, a functional prototype is required (worth 2.5 points) to demonstrate the realization of project requirements.

- Can be built using web technologies (HTML/JS), low-code tools, or high-fidelity UI/UX prototyping tools (Figma with interactions).
- Must demonstrate at least 3 core functionalities mentioned in the topic description.

## C. Presentation

- A 15-minute presentation followed by a Q&amp;A session.
- Demonstration of the prototype.
- Summary of management challenges faced during the project.

## 4. Required Tools

Students are expected to use and show evidence of the following tools in their report:

- Scheduling: Microsoft Project or equivalent.

- Task Management: JIRA, Trello or equivalent.

- Documentation: Professional report format (PDF).
