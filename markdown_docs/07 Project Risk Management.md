## The Concept of Risk

## Risk = f(Likelihood, Impact)

Projects are inherently uncertain.

Outcomes differ from plans.

Manage unknown to survive.

Likelihood: Probability of occurrence.

Impact: Severity of consequence.

Risk = Likelihood x Impact.

Step 2: Assess Risks.

Step 4: Track Control.

## Dimension 1: Risk Likelihood

- Probability of a risk materializing.
- Measured on 0 to 1.0 scale.
- Can use qualitative rating scales.
- Common ratings: High, Medium, Low.
- Estimates rely on expert judgment.
- Combine opinions for better accuracy.

## The Risk Register Framework

- Log all identified project risks.
- Sort by highest consequence rating.
- Focus on moderate-to-high risks.
- Assign specific risk owners.
- Develop targeted mitigation strategies.
- Never ignore severe impact risks.
