## The Concept of Risk

## Risk=f(Likelihood,Impact)

Projects are inherently uncertain.

Outcomes differ from plans.

Manage unknown to survive.

Likelihood: Probability of occurrence.

Impact: Severity of consequence.

Risk = Likelihood x Impact.

## Step 2:Risk Assessment

- Likelihood: Probability of occurrence.
- Impact: Severity of damage.
- Measured in time delay.
- Measured in cost overrun.
- High risk demands attention.
- Calculate Expected Value consequences.

<!-- image -->

<!-- image -->

## Dimension 1: Risk Likelihood

- Probability of a risk materializing.
- Measured on 0 to 1.0 scale.
- Can use qualitative rating scales.
- Common ratings: High, Medium, Low.
- Estimates rely on expert judgment.
- Combine opinions for better accuracy.

<!-- image -->

<!-- image -->

## Dimension 2: Risk Impact

<!-- image -->

- Consequences if risk actually happens.
- Severely affects time, cost, performance.
- Measured on 0 to 1.0 scale.
- Rated as High, Medium, Low.
- Assess without planned preventive measures.
- Evaluate worst-case scenario impacts.

## Calculating Risk Consequence

<!-- image -->

- Combines likelihood and impact together.
- RCR ranges from 0 to 1.0.
- High RCR demands immediate attention.
- Used to compare and prioritize.

## The Risk Register Framework

- Log all identified project risks.
- Sort by highest consequence rating.
- Focus on moderate-to-high risks.
- Assign specific risk owners.
- Develop targeted mitigation strategies.
- Never ignore severe impact risks.

## Step 3: Response Planning

<!-- image -->

- Transfer: Shift to others.
- Avoid: Change project plan.
- Reduce:Lowerlikelihood impact.
- Accept: Acknowledge and proceed.
- Contingency: Plan backup actions.
- Match strategy to risk.

<!-- image -->

## Strategy Group 1: Proactive Action

<!-- image -->

## AVOID:

- Eliminate the risk source entirely.
- Change project scope or schedule.

<!-- image -->

## REDUCE:

- Lower likelihood or impact level.
- Test early, use better resources.
